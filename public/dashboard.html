      How Ugandan primary grading is usually reported

      At PLE, UNEB gives each examined subject a grade from 1 (best) to 9. Those grades are added into an aggregate (best total 4, highest 36). Division I–IV (and U) describe overall performance from that total aggregate, not from one subject in isolation.

      This screen is for school continuous assessment (e.g. marks out of 100). Your Settings scale converts that percentage into a UNEB-style subject grade and remarks. Division (DIV) is then derived the usual way: from the combined grades across subjects (equivalent 4-subject aggregate → Div I–IV or U), not from a single subject’s row on the scale.

      Type only marks scored (out of 100 for each subject). The system fills grade (1 = best), remark, and your initials from Settings (grading scale + display name). Overall division is computed once from all subject grades — it is not part of each subject’s saved row — nothing else to type.

      Primary marks — grading scale

      Each row is an inclusive percentage range (marks scored out of 100, i.e. 0–100). When a learner’s mark falls in a row, the Comments workspace fills AGG (we suggest UNEB-style 1–9, 1 = best) and Remark. Division (DIV) on mark sheets is not edited here — it is computed from the total of those digit grades across subjects (PLE-style equivalent aggregate). Keep rows contiguous and non-overlapping.
